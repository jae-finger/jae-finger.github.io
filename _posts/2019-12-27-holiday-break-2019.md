### December / Holiday Break 2019
The holiday break falls at the tail end of my first unit with Lambda School. Unit 1 in the data science program introduced story telling and prepping data for cleaning; statistical theory and tests; basic visalization packages; and linear algebra. The unit is not over yet, though.
During the first week of January, my cohort is working on their first _build sprint_. A build sprint is a two-week long project that we build. This first project involves
writing and posting an article with a research question and visualizations that we create. 

So far, my projet is built from a new dataset of survey questions asked to [Ph.D. students in 2019](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-019-03459-7). I'm excited to be able to add
a project to my closet. I know it won't be anything special as (a) I don't know a lot about styling in data science yet, so the plots may be cruder than expected and
(b)school projects don't have the same weight as in indepdendent project. However, hopefully I can return to this project later in the future to add styling/polish and to find new projects
to add in the future. I have done a rudimentary cleaning of the data and am working on a second visualization. Here is a rough version of my first visualization depicting how much
one-on-one time Ph.D. students felt they got per week. The colors of the bar represent those students that did and dit not seek help for anxiety and depression too. Based on first glimpse,
it looks like that the percentage of students seeking help for anxiety and depression remains constant despite the amount of 1:1 time with their advisor. I may also remove the 'other' category, which I added
based on recommendation from my team lead.
